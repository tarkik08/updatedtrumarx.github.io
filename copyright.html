What is Copyright?

A legal right granted exclusively to the creator of an original work.

Scope of Protection

Copyright is a legal right given exclusively to the creator/originator (or assignee) of a work. It includes the right to reproduce, publish, perform, translate, and adapt the work in any material form.

"Work" includes:

  • Literary works (including computer programs)
  • Artistic works (paintings, sculptures, drawings)
  • Dramatic and musical works
  • Cinematographic films and sound recordings

"The protection and safeguards provided by copyright are conducive to the reward of creativity. This protection induces creators to create more and motivates others to contribute to the world of art and literature."

Why Register Your Copyright?

While protection exists from creation, registration provides critical legal advantages.

Prima Facie Evidence

A Certificate of Registration serves as prima facie evidence in a court of law regarding ownership disputes.

Legal Remedies

Registration enables the owner to seek civil and criminal remedies against offenders for infringement.

Long Duration

Protection lasts for the author's life + 60 years. For other works, it is 60 years from publication.
