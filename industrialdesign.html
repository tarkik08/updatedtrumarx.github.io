What is an Industrial Design?

Industrial design protects how your product looks its shape, pattern, color, or unique features. It's all about visual appeal, not function.

Unlike patents, this is for non-functional design only. Can't protect moving parts or tech. Just the aesthetic that makes customers choose your product.

Locarno Classification

Design rights can be claimed for almost all objects manufactured industrially. India follows the Locarno Classification of Articles to categorize products for design registration.

Legal Standard

Section 2(ac) of the Patents Act defines industrial application as the capability of being made or used in an industry. The invention must have practical utility.

Enabling Disclosure

A critical criterion for protection is the "disclosure of an enabling patent". The draft specification must disclose the invention sufficiently to enable a skilled person to carry it out without undue effort. If the specification is insufficient, the patent/right will not be granted.

Requirements in India

  1. The Design must have its own novelty or uniqueness and not be in the public domain anywhere.
  2. It should be applied or applicable to an existing product.
  3. The design must be applied using an Industrial process.
  4. Functional or mechanical parts cannot be registered.
  5. It cannot include Trademarks or Copyrighted content (artistic works like paintings defined under Copyright Act 1957).
  6. Design rights are valid for 10 years, extendable by 5 years.
